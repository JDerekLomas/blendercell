Syncytium Network Structure

A multinucleated syncytium: A single giant cell containing thousands of nuclei distributed throughout a continuous cytoplasm with no internal cell walls.

Key Features Visualized:

  • Yellow Tubes: Contractile veins forming L-system branching network
  • Yellow Dots: Thousands of nuclei (DNA) positioned along veins
  • Orange Nodes: Mitochondrial clusters at high-energy zones
  • Shuttle Streaming: Bidirectional cytoplasmic flow through veins
  • Murray's Law: Branching diameters follow optimal scaling (r³ = 2r₁³ + 2r₂³)

Protoplasmic Streaming: The cytoplasm pulses through the contractile veins in a shuttle-like rhythm, transporting nutrients and distributing chemical signals. This rhythmic flow is how the syncytium "thinks" without a brain.

Heather Barnett's Work: Barnett documented that Physarum can solve mazes, optimize networks (recreating Tokyo's railway system), and make foraging decisions—all through the physics of protoplasmic streaming and distributed decision-making across multiple nuclei.
